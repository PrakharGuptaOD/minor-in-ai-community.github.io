AI Community Managed By Volunteers

Guidelines for Members

Before we begin, I request everyone to go through each section and point of this document carefully. If you have any questions, please check the FAQ section of this document first. Any kind of negligence or nonchalant behavior will not be tolerated!

This document is divided into multiple sections, all of which are listed below. You can visit any of the sections directly by clicking on it. However, it is highly recommended to go through each section carefully and sequentially.

  • Things you must Avoid!
  • Things you must Do!
  • Groups and their Purpose!
  • Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Things You Must Avoid :

  1. Avoid sending direct messages to any community members, admins, or volunteers, especially Sudarshan Sir or Meenakshi Ma’am, unless you have explicit permission from the respective person.
  2. Avoid using unprofessional, foul, or vulgar language or getting engaged in any unnecessary debate, in any of the community groups. Such behavior will not be tolerated.
  3. Avoid sending irrelevant messages to unrelated groups. Each group in this community has a specific purpose, and details about each group are provided in this document.

Things you Must do :

  1. You must be respectful towards all community members, whether you know them personally or not. Treating your peers with respect is a key trait of a professional individual.
  2. You must follow the specified format for sending messages in certain groups, as some groups have specific texting guidelines. Adhering to these formats will be appreciated by everyone and will help reduce unnecessary traffic in the groups. The format is mentioned below in the document.
  3. You must react to every announcement you see in the community's announcement channel. This helps volunteers gauge how many members have read the announcement.
  4. You must read this document thoroughly and follow all the rules. Once you have completed reading, react with a "thumbs up" emoji in the announcement channel to confirm.
  5. You must set your full name as your WhatsApp username for easy networking. Additionally, having a professional profile picture and including your LinkedIn handle in your "About" section is recommended to maintain professionalism and enhance career opportunities.
  6. You must contribute to the community, not just consume. Engage meaningfully, participate actively, and help build an AI alumni network that others will admire.

Groups and their purpose:

Before proceeding with this section, please ensure that you join only the groups that align with your interests. Avoid joining all groups unnecessarily.

  1. General Group : The first group of this community is meant solely for initiating general discussions. Any questions related to academics, LMS, or assignments are strictly prohibited. Before asking any other question, please ensure that it is not already addressed in the FAQs section of this document. 

When initiating a discussion, make sure to follow the specified format.

(format is mentioned below!)

        <Greetings>

        <topic of discussion>

        <urgency on a scale of 1-10>

        <breakout room between 1-4 or voice>

        Example :

        

        Hello, everyone!

This is Prakhar. I would like to discuss "RAG" as I plan to implement it in one of my personal projects. The urgency of this discussion is 3/10. If you're interested, please react with a 👍 on this message, and we will continue the discussion in Breakout Room 4.

Thank you!        

Members are allowed to send only one message that covers all relevant points. Those interested in the discussion must react with a thumbs-up emoji on the initial message. The discussion will then be moved to the designated breakout room.

Try to avoid sending additional messages or creating a thread. If your thread in the general group exceeds five messages, it will be forcefully moved from the general channel to one of the breakout rooms.

  1. Breakout Groups : This community has 4 text-based breakout rooms and 1 voice-based breakout room. Open discussions are allowed in these rooms, but all discussions must comply with community guidelines.

Each breakout room will have appointed volunteers responsible for moderation, along with super moderators overseeing all discussions. Please avoid unnecessary discussions and ensure that all conversations remain respectful and meaningful.

  1. Special Groups : There are several special groups in the community, and their guidelines are provided in the group description itself. Please refer to them.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

  • Community Rules & Eligibility
  • Q: What are the eligibility requirements to join this community?

A: To be eligible for membership, individuals must either be currently enrolled in the Minor in AI course or have plans to enroll shortly.

  • Q: Is this the official Minor in AI group?

A: No, this group is a community-led initiative managed by volunteers.

  • Q: Who are the volunteers?

A: The volunteers are students from various cohorts of the Minor in AI course, selected through a thorough and thoughtful discussion process.

  • Q: Can I become a volunteer?

A: Yes, if you demonstrate potential and actively contribute, we can consider adding you as a volunteer.

  • Q: Who has the authority to manage these groups?

A: The groups are managed by volunteers under the able guidance and authority of the respected Dr. Sudarshan Iyengar.

  • Q: Can I be removed from the group?

A: Yes, if you do not follow the community guidelines then you may be removed.

  • Q: I am not part of any Minor in AI group, nor do I wish to join any AI activities. Can I still be in this group?

A: No, please avoid wasting your valuable time. Kindly leave this group. This group is dedicated to AI-related discussions.

  • Q: I do not see any useful activity happening that benefits me. What should I do?

A: Please share your interests and suggestions in the group. We will make every effort to ensure that your points are addressed.

  • Group Behavior & Etiquette
  • Q: Why are there so many breakout rooms? Why can't I write freely in the General or other groups?

A: Breakout rooms help keep discussions focused and organized without overwhelming others in the General group.

  • Q: Can I send general messages like "hello," "hi," or birthday messages in the group?

A: No. Please ensure your messages are relevant to the group context only.

  • Q: Are there any time restrictions for messaging within the group?

A: While the group is accessible 24/7, please avoid sending unnecessary messages at night to respect everyone's time.

  • Q: I need to have a long discussion on a specific topic. What should I do?

A: Move to a breakout room for extended discussions.

  • Issue Reporting & Conflict Resolution
  • Q: Whom should I report to if I notice something wrong?

A: You should report any issues to the volunteers within that group.

  • Q: What should I do if someone messages me directly during the day/night without my approval?

A: Please inform us, and we will investigate the matter and take strict action, including blocking the person if necessary.

  • Q: What should I do if I am invited by members of the group to some suspicious chat groups?

A: Please inform us, and we will investigate the matter and take strict action, including blocking the individuals involved.

  • Q: What should I do if I receive a vulgar or inappropriate message?

A: Please inform us immediately. We will investigate the matter, take strict action, block the sender, and report the incident to the authorities.

  • Q: What should I do if a volunteer acts rudely by enforcing strict rules?

A: Please read the community guidelines thoroughly. If you are not following them, it is the responsibility of the volunteers to monitor and enforce these guidelines.

  • Q: Can I ask for another chance after being removed from a group? What steps should I follow to get back in?

A: While we acknowledge that mistakes happen, it is important to try and avoid them in the first place. However, we will try to be compassionate if the person is genuinely sincere. Please express your sincerity and explain the situation to us, and we will consider your request.

  • MASAI-Related Queries
  • Q: When will I receive my email ID from IIT Ropar?

A: Please raise a ticket with Masai to inquire about your email ID.

  • Q: What should I do if MASAI is not answering my queries?

A: This platform is not affiliated with MASAI. However, you can raise a ticket in the LMS for assistance.

  • Q: I want to change my batch, but no one is listening, and they are asking for a huge fee. What should I do?

A: This is not an official MASAI group. Please take this issue up with MASAI directly by raising a ticket on the LMS.

  • Q: Why doesn’t Masai or the Masters respond to queries about batch changes, T-shirts, or campus orientation?

A: While you can raise a ticket for your concerns, there are regular support sessions happening almost every other day. Please check Slack and join those sessions to get your queries addressed.

  • Learning Opportunities
  • Q: I wish to volunteer for a free training session on a specific topic. Can I conduct it in a group?

A: Please tag the volunteers after posting your intention, and we will notify you soon.

  • Q: Can this community help me get a job?

A: This community is a platform for networking, knowledge sharing and discussions, not for job placements.

  • Q: Can I get an internship at IIT Ropar or participate in a Campus Immersion?

A: This decision is made by Masai and IIT Ropar officials. Please write to them for further information.

  • General Support & Troubleshooting
  • Q: What should I do if I ask questions and no one is replying?

A: Volunteers are students just like you. They may be busy or have exams, so please be patient.

  • Q: The above guidelines and FAQs do not address my issue. What should I do?

A: Please let us know about your concern, and we will get back to you soon. We appreciate your cooperation and time.
